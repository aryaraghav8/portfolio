Legal-Tech UX Case Study

Simplifying legal workflows through a structured document management platform.

Designing a unified legal workflow system that reduced operational complexity, improved onboarding, and aligned digital experiences with real legal processes.

Role
UX Designer
Timeline
3–4 Months
Platform
Enterprise Web App
Overview

The challenge

Legal professionals were managing client communication, document submissions, and workflow tracking across disconnected tools including spreadsheets, email threads, and physical files.

The challenge was creating a centralized platform without overwhelming users unfamiliar with modern enterprise software systems.

“Legal users think in milestones and case stages — not software structures.”

Research

Understanding legal mental models

Conducted workflow discussions and task mapping sessions to understand how legal professionals organize information, track cases, and manage documentation.

Fragmented workflows

Case progress was spread across multiple disconnected systems and communication channels.

High onboarding friction

Users found enterprise software intimidating and difficult to learn.

Complex document handling

Uploading, categorizing, and tracking legal files required excessive manual effort.

Early workflow architecture and legal process mapping.
Process

Structuring workflows around legal milestones

Instead of designing around backend structures, the platform was organized around how legal teams naturally think about case progression.

Step 01 — Workflow restructuring

Mapped the interface around legal stages: intake → review → submission → resolution.

Step 02 — Guided onboarding

Introduced contextual onboarding and progressive disclosure patterns.

Step 03 — Smarter document handling

Designed batch upload flows with automatic categorization suggestions.

Wireframes and legal workflow restructuring explorations.
Design System

Building scalable enterprise patterns

Created reusable patterns for dashboards, document states, workflow tracking, and onboarding interactions to improve consistency across the platform.

Reusable workflow components

Timeline systems, document cards, progress states, filters, and action panels.

Frontend-aware design decisions

Component structures aligned with responsive implementation and scalable layouts.

Outcomes

Reducing complexity across legal operations

The redesigned workflow system improved visibility across case progress, simplified onboarding, and reduced operational friction for legal teams.

Reflection

Key learnings

This project reinforced how important information architecture and mental model alignment are in enterprise workflow systems.

Simplifying legal complexity wasn’t about removing functionality — it was about structuring information in a way users already understood intuitively.
