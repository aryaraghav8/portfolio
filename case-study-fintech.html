Education + Fintech UX Case Study

Redesigning conversion-focused learning journeys for a global finance certification platform.

Worked on multiple high-impact pages across the AAFM India ecosystem including homepage redesigns, certification pages, accreditation flows, and conversion-focused experiences to improve clarity, trust, and course discoverability.

Overview

The challenge

AAFM India offers multiple globally recognized finance certifications and professional learning programs. However, the platform experience contained fragmented navigation, dense informational layouts, and inconsistent visual hierarchy across key pages.

Users exploring certifications such as Chartered Wealth Manager (CWM®) often faced difficulty understanding eligibility, certification pathways, career outcomes, and program structure due to content-heavy interfaces.

The platform also required stronger trust-focused design patterns and clearer conversion flows to improve user confidence and inquiry generation.

"The goal wasn't just redesigning pages — it was creating a more structured and trustworthy learning ecosystem for finance professionals."

Research

Understanding finance education user journeys

Conducted flow analysis and UX audits across course discovery, informational pages, and inquiry journeys to understand where users experienced confusion, hesitation, or drop-offs.

01

Information overload

Users struggled to scan long-form course content and certification details efficiently.

02

Important sections such as career outcomes, eligibility, and accreditation lacked visual emphasis.

03

Trust visibility issues

Global recognition, accreditation, and institutional credibility were not sufficiently highlighted.

04

Inconsistent navigation patterns

Users moving between course pages and supporting content experienced fragmented structures.

Reflection

Key learnings

This project strengthened my understanding of designing for education and finance ecosystems where users require both informational depth and decision-making confidence.

Working across multiple interconnected pages helped me think beyond isolated screens and focus on scalable systems, structured content hierarchy, and conversion-focused UX.

The experience also improved my ability to balance business goals, trust-building, and frontend-aware implementation within content-heavy interfaces.
